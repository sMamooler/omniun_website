Title: Poetry and Music Converge for Literacy: Evening Verse Gala Inspires Advocacy Projects
Date: October 15, 2025
The 2025 Evening Verse Gala, scheduled for October 22 and organized by the Horizon Literary Network, is set to make an impact far beyond its four-hour cultural showcase. Contributors to the event, such as poet Saalem Darris and guitarist Hana Melvie, are extending the gala’s mission into underserved communities with plans for voluntary workshops and proceeds benefiting literacy programs. Saalem Darris, known for his works reflecting rural life and endurance, has announced plans to visit Norvalla later this year to host poetry workshops. These workshops will focus on helping participants use poetry as a tool for self-expression, particularly in addressing themes of resilience and rural life, aligning closely with the gala’s mission of empowerment through the arts.
Hana Melvie, celebrated for her evocative guitar compositions, revealed that proceeds from her upcoming release, "Resilient Strings," will be partially donated to literacy initiatives. The composition, which celebrates endurance and hope, also features a bonus track inspired by her experiences teaching music to children at a community center, further tying her work to the cause of literacy and education. Melvie has encouraged fellow artists to leverage their influence for advocacy, emphasizing a shared commitment to the gala's goals of fostering education and the arts as transformative tools.
PagePath Connect, an organization focused on improving literacy rates in underserved communities, praised the dedication shown by Darris and Melvie. The organization also announced plans to collaborate with the Horizon Literary Network on future literacy campaigns, building on the solidarity demonstrated by contributors to the gala. With contributors like Darris and Melvie leading the charge, the 2025 Evening Verse Gala is more than an evening of art—it is a movement to empower voices and uplift lives.


Title: "Resilient Ties: Benefit Concert Raises $45,000 for Literacy and the Arts"
Date: 2026-03-15
The Horizon Literary Network and renowned guitarist Hana Melvie joined forces on March 15, 2026, to host a benefit concert entitled "Resilient Ties" at the historic Lillian Vaine Cultural Center. Drawing an enthusiastic audience, the event raised an impressive $45,000 to support literacy initiatives and artistic programs. Proceeds will directly fund rural literacy workshops and the expanded distribution of the "Voices of Wisterr" poetry anthology, a community-driven collection celebrating the creativity and stories of Wisterr's residents. Additionally, PagePath Connect announced plans to use part of the funds to create interactive literacy kits for schools and libraries, featuring poetry prompts, musical activities, and materials inspired by the anthology. Held in partnership with PagePath Connect and the Wisterr Cultural Council, the concert illustrated the power of art and music to foster resilience and connection.
PagePath Connect also confirmed that the funds raised would support the launch of two new mobile libraries in Serillia, aimed at enhancing access to creative and literary resources in underserved communities. These mobile libraries will include a dedicated section for beginner guides to poetry and music, as well as recordings of performances from the "Resilient Ties" concert to inspire local artists. This initiative reflects the event's broader mission to empower individuals through the arts and expand opportunities for creative expression.
Further extending the impact of the event, the Wisterr Cultural Council revealed plans to host a series of workshops in Wisterr later this year, focusing on themes of community and mentorship. Led by mentor artists from the concert, these sessions will offer hands-on experiences in poetry, music composition, and storytelling. Participants will be encouraged to draw inspiration from their local environment and personal journeys, fostering a deeper connection to their creative work. The event's success has underscored the enduring value of collaboration and grassroots initiatives in empowering both individuals and communities through the arts.
