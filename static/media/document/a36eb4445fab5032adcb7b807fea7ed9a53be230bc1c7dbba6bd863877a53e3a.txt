Title: "Voices of Wisterr" Anthology Celebrates Rural Resilience Through Poetry
Date: 2026-01-15
On January 15, 2026, poet Saalem Darris announced the completion of "Voices of Wisterr," an anthology of poetry created with contributions from participants of his poetry workshop in the rural village of Wisterr. The collection features 27 poems that delve into themes of rural resilience, personal endurance, and the vibrant community life in Wisterr. Contributors to the anthology include farmers, students, and artists, each offering a unique perspective on the shared experiences and stories rooted in their close-knit village.
The anthology opens with a foreword by Darris, where he reflects on the transformative power of poetry in connecting individuals and preserving the stories of rural life. Among the contributors is Merryn Skove, a high school student whose poem explores the journey of finding one's voice within a close-knit community. Speaking at the event, Skove credited Darris’s mentorship for inspiring her to embrace creative self-expression. Farmer and poet Arlen Tovey also shared his pride in contributing to the collection, describing his poem as a heartfelt portrayal of his enduring connection to the land and the seasonal struggles that shape his life.
The launch of "Voices of Wisterr" was celebrated as a significant moment for Wisterr’s creative community, highlighting the shared stories and artistic contributions of its residents. Darris, known for his reflective poetry inspired by rural life, expressed his hope that the anthology would serve as both a celebration of Wisterr’s unique heritage and an invitation for others to draw strength from the written word. The project reflects Darris's belief in the power of collaborative creativity to connect individuals and honor the beauty of small-town life in Norvalla.


Title: "Resilient Ties" Brings Artists and Mentors Together at Lillian Vaine Cultural Center
Date: 2026-03-15
On March 15, 2026, the Horizon Literary Network, in collaboration with Sandrian guitarist Hana Melvie, hosted a heartwarming benefit concert titled "Resilient Ties" at the historic Lillian Vaine Cultural Center. The event celebrated the transformative power of mentorship programs that nurture creativity and resilience in the arts. Featuring an array of artistic performances, the concert brought together artists from diverse backgrounds to honor the enduring bonds forged through shared artistic journeys.
Each performance was introduced with a narrative about the artist's personal growth, offering insights into how mentorship shaped their creative paths and personal development. Norvallan poet Saalem Darris delivered a standout moment with his original poem, "Echoes of Growth," written specifically for the occasion. The poem, inspired by Darris's experiences mentoring young poets in rural communities, used vivid metaphors of seeds growing into towering trees to reflect on the enduring impact of mentorship and the artistic resilience it fosters. His heartfelt and evocative performance earned a standing ovation from the audience.
In a post-event interview with Strata Media, Hana Melvie described "Resilient Ties" as "an emotional bridge connecting artists across generations." She expressed gratitude to her mentors and collaborators, emphasizing how the concert served as a testament to the power of mentorship in the creative arts. Melvie also shared that the event was a deeply personal milestone for her, as it marked the first time she performed alongside one of her own mentees, Ardan Evale, in a professional setting. The success of the evening reinforced the Horizon Literary Network's mission to champion literary and artistic communities, leaving attendees and participants inspired by its message of resilience and growth.
