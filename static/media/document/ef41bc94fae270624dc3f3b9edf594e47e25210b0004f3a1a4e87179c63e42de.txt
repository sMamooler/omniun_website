Title: Selvia Renek Hosts SnapGram Q&A to Address Ethical Certification Concerns
Date: July 21, 2024
On July 21, 2024, food blogger Selvia Renek hosted a live SnapGram Q&A session to address public concerns about ethical certification criteria proposed by the Calder Square Cultural Committee. The session followed a public forum held on July 10 at Alveris Community Hall, where committee members introduced plans to revise the program based on feedback from local vendors, artisans, and residents. Renek began the session by summarizing key points from the forum, emphasizing the committee's efforts to align ethical sourcing standards with the economic realities of small businesses in the community.
During the Q&A, Renek addressed specific concerns raised by small business owners, particularly about the financial burden of audits linked to the certification process. She explained that the committee is exploring phased implementation strategies to help vendors transition more smoothly into compliance. Additionally, she shared insights about a proposed tiered certification system, which could reward businesses exceeding baseline ethical standards with benefits such as reduced event participation fees. Renek also highlighted feedback from the Varentian Ethical Sourcing Alliance, which has advocated for transparency measures like a public database of certified vendors, even as the committee considers relaxing some audit requirements to accommodate small businesses.
Following the interactive session, Renek thanked her audience in a SnapGram post for their active participation and encouraged them to contribute further by taking part in the Calder Square Cultural Committee’s ongoing digital survey. The survey aims to gather broader community input, ensuring that the evolving certification program reflects the values and concerns of local stakeholders. This initiative underscores both Renek and the committee's shared commitment to fostering inclusive dialogues about ethical business practices while supporting the sustainability of small enterprises across Alveris.


Title: Self-Certification Program at Calder Square Sparks Community Dialogue
Date: August 1, 2024
The Calder Square Cultural Committee has introduced a self-certification program for local vendors, aiming to foster inclusivity and ethical practices at community food events. In a detailed blog post, Varentian food blogger Selvia Renek covered the program’s rollout, including interviews with committee representatives and participating vendors. Vendors expressed optimism about the initiative, with one stating, “This feels like a step in the right direction for small businesses like mine to be part of Calder Square’s vibrant events.” Renek’s blog highlighted the program’s potential to create opportunities for smaller vendors and enhance Calder Square’s role in celebrating Varentia’s cultural and culinary heritage.
Amara Hearth Café, a boutique coffeehouse, announced its participation in the program’s workshops as part of its ongoing efforts to incorporate ethically sourced ingredients into its menu. Co-owner Erena Treflin shared that the first workshop inspired her team to explore partnerships with local farmers for their upcoming autumn menu. The café plans to feature new seasonal offerings, including spiced apple tarts and pumpkin chai muffins, crafted with a focus on sustainability and community collaboration. Treflin emphasized that these efforts align with the café’s mission to support local artisans and foster stronger ties with the community.
Community feedback collected through a digital survey conducted by the Calder Square Cultural Committee revealed mixed reactions. While many residents expressed optimism about the program’s potential to increase representation for smaller vendors at future food events, others noted a desire for a more diverse range of food offerings that reflect Alveris’s multicultural identity. Some residents also expressed hope that the self-certification option would encourage greater inclusivity and innovation in the city’s culinary scene. Despite varied opinions, the program has sparked meaningful dialogue about ethical sourcing and inclusivity, with vendors and café owners optimistic about its potential to enrich Calder Square’s events.
