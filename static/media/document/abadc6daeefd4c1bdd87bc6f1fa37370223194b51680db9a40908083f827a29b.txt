Title: Horizon Literary Network Unveils Inspiring Line-Up for 2025 Evening Verse Gala
Date: 2025-02-28
The Horizon Literary Network gathered supporters and media on February 28 at the historic Lillian Vaine Cultural Center to announce its plans for the 2025 Evening Verse Gala. This year’s event will spotlight emerging poets and musicians from underserved communities, aiming to celebrate their creative voices and share their stories of resilience. Central to this announcement were two new contributors: Saalem Darris, a poet from Wisterr who grew up in a small farming community, and Hana Melvie, a guitarist from Auraline Heights who began her musical journey on a borrowed guitar from a local community center. Both artists have overcome significant socioeconomic challenges, embodying the gala's mission to uplift and support talent from diverse backgrounds.
Saalem Darris shared how writing poetry became a way to process the struggles of his upbringing and connect with others who shared similar experiences. Meanwhile, Hana Melvie recounted how the borrowed guitar sparked her passion for music and storytelling, ultimately shaping her artistic journey. Their stories reflect the transformative power of the arts and align with Horizon Literary Network’s mission to expand access to literature and the arts while fostering a culture of mentorship and creativity.
The press event concluded with a call for sponsorships and ticket sales for the 2025 Evening Verse Gala, with all proceeds to be reinvested into additional mentorship programs and literacy initiatives spearheaded by the Horizon Literary Network. Organizers also revealed that early ticket sales had already surpassed expectations, reflecting strong community support for the event and its mission. With a prestigious venue like the Lillian Vaine Cultural Center and a focus on amplifying underrepresented voices, the 2025 Evening Verse Gala is poised to be a meaningful celebration of talent and a platform for social impact.


Title: "Resilient Ties" Benefit Concert Celebrates Artistic Collaboration and Mentorship
Date: 2026-03-15
The Horizon Literary Network, in collaboration with acclaimed Sandrian guitarist Hana Melvie, hosted the "Resilient Ties" benefit concert on March 15, 2026, at the Lillian Vaine Cultural Center. This vibrant event brought together emerging and seasoned artists to highlight the transformative power of mentorship and artistic resilience. Featuring performances from contributors involved in literacy mentorship programs funded by PagePath Connect, the concert offered a heartfelt exploration of intergenerational collaboration and the enduring impact of access to the literary and performing arts.
A standout moment of the evening was a stirring duet of Hana Melvie’s evocative composition, "Strings of Resolve," performed alongside emerging guitarist Ardan Evale. Evale, a rising talent from Serillia, honed his craft through a PagePath Connect-funded community workshop, showcasing the program's success in fostering new talent. The dynamic interplay between Evale’s youthful energy and Melvie’s seasoned expertise was met with a standing ovation from the audience, underscoring the evening's themes of growth and perseverance. Poet Saalem Darris, who hails from the rural village of Wisterr, contributed with an original piece titled "Echoes of Growth," written specifically for the event. His poignant reading reflected on the profound ways mentorship fosters artistic and personal resilience, further deepening the evening’s emotional resonance.
The night closed on an intimate and reflective note with a surprise performance by acclaimed Callisian poet Claudia Arlens. Accompanied by Hana Melvie’s gentle guitar melodies, Arlens performed "Threads of Light," a spoken-word piece exploring the theme of finding hope in shared stories. The synergy between Arlens’s vivid storytelling and Melvie's emotive accompaniment captivated the audience, leaving an enduring impression of the evening's central message: the transformative power of art and community. The "Resilient Ties" concert not only celebrated artistic expression but also amplified the importance of programs like those sponsored by PagePath Connect in fostering creativity across generations.
